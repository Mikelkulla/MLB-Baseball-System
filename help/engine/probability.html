Probability Engine

How raw American odds are converted to vig-free true probabilities, then adjusted by injuries, pitching, and bullpen quality.

Step 1 — Vig Removal

Bookmakers embed a margin (the "vig" or "juice") into their lines. Both raw implied probabilities sum to more than 100%. We remove this overround before any analysis.

Raw Implied Probability from American Odds

American → Implied
If odds ≥ 0: raw_prob = 100 / (odds + 100)
If odds < 0: raw_prob = |odds| / (|odds| + 100)

Remove the Overround

Vig-Free Probability
total = raw_away + raw_home # will be > 1.0 (e.g. 1.05 = 5% vig)
true_away = raw_away / total × 100
true_home = raw_home / total × 100

# Example: -150 / -130
raw_away = 0.600, raw_home = 0.565, total = 1.165 (16.5% vig)
true_away = 51.5%, true_home = 48.5%

Result: two true probabilities that always sum to exactly 100%.

Step 2 — Injury Adjustment

See Injury Impact for how the delta is calculated. The adjustment uses a net effect so the sum stays at 100%.

Injury Probability Adjustment
net = home_injury_delta − away_injury_delta
new_away = away_prob + net / 2
new_home = home_prob − net / 2
# Re-normalise to sum = 100 after shift

Sign convention: injury_impact is stored as a negative number when a team is hurt. A team missing its closer (impact = −2.0) hurts them → opponent's probability rises.

Step 3 — Pitcher Adjustment

When a dominant ace faces a weak starter, the model shifts probability independently from the market. Maximum shift is ±10 percentage points (scaled by park factor).

Pitcher Probability Adjustment
edge = away_pitcher_score − home_pitcher_score # range −100 to +100
prob_shift = (edge / 50.0) × 5.0 × park_scaling # max ±10pp × park_scaling
new_away = away_prob + prob_shift
new_home = home_prob − prob_shift
# Re-normalise to sum = 100

Both pitchers at 50 (spring training / TBD) → edge = 0 → no shift.

Park scaling: park_scaling = 2.0 − park_factor, clamped [0.60, 1.25]. At Coors (factor=1.38): scaling=0.62, reducing the max shift to ±6.2pp.

Step 4 — Bullpen Adjustment

Bullpen handles ~40% of innings. The max adjustment is ±4pp base (proportionally lower than the ±10pp starter adjustment).

Bullpen Probability Adjustment
edge = away_bullpen_score − home_bullpen_score
prob_shift = (edge / 50.0) × 2.0 × park_scaling # max ±4pp × park_scaling
new_away = away_prob + prob_shift
new_home = home_prob − prob_shift
# Re-normalise to sum = 100

Both teams at 50 (spring training / fewer than 10 games) → no shift.

Final Probability Values

After all four steps, away_prob_pct and home_prob_pct represent the model's true estimate of each team's win probability. These are used to calculate EV.

Why probabilities sum to more than market-implied

When pitcher/injury adjustments push one side's probability above market-implied, the EV for that side becomes positive. This is the model's edge — it disagrees with the market in a statistically grounded way. Without adjustments active (e.g. spring training), both EVs equal −(vig%).
