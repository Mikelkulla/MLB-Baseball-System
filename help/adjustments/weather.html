Weather Impact

All O/U adjustments from wind direction, wind speed, temperature, precipitation, altitude, and dome status — all calibrated against large MLB game samples.

Weather affects O/U, not moneyline

Weather adjustments are O/U line adjustments (positive = push toward Over, negative = push toward Under). They do not directly shift win probability. They inform the totals analysis on the MLB Model page.

Wind — Direction & Speed

Wind direction is determined by comparing the meteorological wind direction (degrees) to the stadium's home-plate-to-CF orientation angle. Blowing "out" = from home plate toward CF = carries fly balls.

Temperature

RangeAdjustmentExplanation
≤ 40°F+1.0 OverCounter-intuitive: OVER hits ~57%. Pitcher grip/command loss in extreme cold dominates bat speed reduction. Documented by multiple MLB sample studies.
41–55°F+0.75 UnderCool range: bat speed reduction dominates. Reduced hard contact, fewer extra-base hits.
56–84°FNo adjustmentNeutral zone. No significant documented effect.
85–89°F+0.75 OverWarm air, better ball carry. Modest Over lean.
≥ 90°F+1.25 OverVery hot: 13–18% more runs vs cold conditions documented. Ball carries furthest.
Extreme cold is an Over signal — this is correct

Many people assume cold = Under. Research shows the opposite at temperatures below 40°F: pitcher grip and command issues cause more walks, wild pitches, and hit batsmen — increasing scoring even as bat speed drops. The Under signal only applies in the cool range (41–55°F) where bat speed reduction dominates without the extreme cold grip effects.

Precipitation

CategoryAdjustmentResearch basis
Light rain+0.5 OverPitcher grip issues in wet conditions → 3.6% more runs on average (documented). More walks, wild pitches, hit batsmen.
Heavy rain+2.0 UnderGame pace disruption, slower fielding, extended innings → reduced run-scoring opportunities.
NoneNo adjustment
Light rain is an Over signal — this is correct

Light rain does NOT suppress scoring. The pitcher struggles to grip the ball → more walks → more baserunners → more runs. Only heavy rain (game-disrupting) suppresses run totals.

Altitude

AltitudeAdjustmentParks affected
≥ 4,000 ft+2.0 OverCoors Field (5,197 ft) — park factor 125–128 vs baseline 100. ~2 extra runs per game.

Note: The altitude adjustment for Coors is handled here in weather_impact.py. The park factor table (ballpark_factors.py) explicitly excludes Coors from its O/U adjustment to prevent double-counting.

Adjustments are Additive

Multiple conditions can fire in the same game. For example: a 90°F game at Coors with 12 mph wind blowing out would accumulate:

very_hot_temp:    +1.25 Over
altitude:         +2.00 Over
wind_out_moderate: +2.00 Over
───────────────────────────
Total:            +5.25 Over
