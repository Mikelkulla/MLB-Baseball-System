Park Factors

30-park run factor table, how park factor scales pitcher/bullpen probability adjustments, and the park O/U adjustment.

What Park Factor Does

Park factors have two roles in the system:

  1. Scale pitching edges — pitcher and bullpen probability adjustments are multiplied by a park scaling factor. In hitter parks, great pitching matters less (the park inflates scoring regardless). In pitcher parks, the edge is amplified.
  2. O/U adjustment — independent of weather, the park's run environment adds or subtracts from the Over/Under signal on the MLB Model page.

Park Pitcher Scaling Formula

Park Scaling Multiplier
scaling = 2.0 − park_factor
scaling = CLAMP(scaling, 0.60, 1.25)

# Coors (1.38) → 2.0 − 1.38 = 0.62 → max ±6.2pp pitcher shift
# Fenway (1.10) → 2.0 − 1.10 = 0.90 → max ±9.0pp
# Neutral(1.00) → 2.0 − 1.00 = 1.00 → max ±10.0pp (unchanged)
# Oracle (0.91) → 2.0 − 0.91 = 1.09 → max ±10.9pp
# Petco (0.88) → 2.0 − 0.88 = 1.12 → max ±11.2pp

Park O/U Adjustment Formula

Park O/U Adjustment
SENSITIVITY = 5.3
park_ou_adj = (park_factor − 1.0) × SENSITIVITY

# Coors (1.38) → excluded (altitude already in weather engine)
# Fenway (1.10) → (1.10 − 1.0) × 5.3 = +0.53 Over
# Petco (0.88) → (0.88 − 1.0) × 5.3 = −0.64 Under
# Oracle (0.91) → (0.91 − 1.0) × 5.3 = −0.48 Under
Coors Field is excluded from park O/U adjustment

The weather_impact.py altitude adjustment already applies +2.0 Over for Coors Field (altitude ≥ 4,000 ft). The park O/U formula would also return +2.01 Over for Coors. To prevent double-counting, park_ou_adjustment_display() returns 0.0 for colorado_rockies.
